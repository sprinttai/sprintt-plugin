# Marketing Strategy

## Guiding Principle

**$0 budget, maximum impact.** Sprintt is bootstrapped with ~$43/mo in operating costs. Every marketing activity must be free or near-free. We trade time and expertise for attention, not dollars.

## Core Strategy: Authority-Led Organic Growth

Build Ricardo's reputation as a practitioner who builds real AI systems — not a thought leader who just talks about them. Every piece of content should either teach something useful or demonstrate a real result.

## Channel Priority (Ranked by ROI for Solo Founder)

### 1. LinkedIn — PRIMARY CHANNEL
**Cost:** $0
**Why:** Decision-makers live here. AI consulting buyers scroll LinkedIn, not TikTok.

**Tactics:**
- Post 3-5x per week (mix of formats below)
- Content types:
  - "Here's what I built this week" — show real work, real results
  - "Most businesses get this wrong about AI" — contrarian takes with substance
  - Breakdowns of how specific AI tools solve specific business problems
  - Design partner journey updates (with permission) — build in public
  - Quick tips that save people time right now
- Engage genuinely on other people's posts (30 min/day)
- Connect with ICP decision-makers with personalized notes (not pitch-slaps)
- Use the Featured section to showcase case studies and the website

**Goal:** 1,000 followers by end of Q2 2026, 3 inbound leads per month by Q3

### 2. X (Twitter) — SECONDARY CHANNEL
**Cost:** $0
**Handle:** @sprinttai
**Why:** Reaches a different slice of the AI/tech audience than LinkedIn. Many builders, founders, and technical decision-makers are active on X but not LinkedIn.

**Low-effort approach — repurpose from LinkedIn, don't create from scratch:**
- Take each LinkedIn post and adapt it for X (shorter, punchier, more direct)
- Threads work well for breakdowns that are too long for a single post
- Engage with AI/product/founder conversations already happening — replies drive more reach than posts at small followings
- Retweet/quote sparingly — original takes get more traction

**What not to do:**
- Don't try to maintain a separate X content strategy — LinkedIn is the source of truth, X is a downstream channel
- Don't feel obligated to post the same volume as LinkedIn

**Goal:** Consistent presence when LinkedIn content is flowing — not a separate workload

---

### 3. Sprintt.ai Website + SEO
**Cost:** $0 (already paying for hosting)
**Why:** Validates credibility when prospects Google you. Also captures organic search traffic over time.

**Tactics:**
- Publish 2-4 blog posts per month on practical AI topics
- Target long-tail keywords: "how to automate [X] with AI," "AI consulting for [industry]"
- Each blog post should answer one specific question a potential client might Google
- Include clear CTAs (book a call, download a guide, subscribe to newsletter)
- Add case studies as they're completed

**Goal:** 500 organic monthly visitors by end of 2026

### 3. Email / Newsletter
**Cost:** $0 (use free tier of Buttondown, Substack, or ConvertKit)
**Why:** Own your audience. LinkedIn can change algorithms. Email is direct.

**Tactics:**
- Weekly or biweekly newsletter: "AI in Practice" — one real use case, one tool recommendation, one actionable tip
- Gate a free resource on the website to build the list (e.g., "AI Readiness Checklist for SMBs")
- Every LinkedIn post should have a subtle CTA to the newsletter
- Keep it short, practical, and personality-forward

**Goal:** 200 subscribers by end of Q2 2026, 500 by end of year

### Other Platforms (Instagram, Threads, TikTok)
Accounts exist at @sprinttai on all three. No active effort planned until LinkedIn and X are producing consistent results. If short-form video becomes a priority later, TikTok and Reels would be the channel to activate. See `company/social-profiles.md` for full platform inventory.

---

### 4. Referral Network
**Cost:** $0
**Why:** Best clients come from referrals, especially in consulting.

**Tactics:**
- Identify 10-15 complementary service providers (accountants, lawyers, marketing agencies, IT managed services) who serve the same clients but don't compete
- Offer to refer clients to them in exchange for reciprocal referrals
- Create a simple one-pager they can share with their clients explaining what Sprintt does
- Make it easy: "If a client says 'we should probably look into AI,' send them my way"

**Goal:** 3-5 active referral partners by end of Q2 2026

### 5. Local / Community Presence
**Cost:** $0-$50 (occasional event tickets)
**Why:** Freeport/Destin/30A area businesses are underserved for AI consulting.

**Tactics:**
- Attend local chamber of commerce events
- Offer to give a free "AI for Your Business" talk at local business groups
- Connect with Emerald Coast tech community if one exists
- Be the local AI person — small pond advantage

**Goal:** 1-2 local speaking opportunities per quarter

## Content Calendar Framework

Don't overplan. Use this as a loose guide:

**Monday:** LinkedIn post — teaching moment or tool tip
**Tuesday:** Engage on LinkedIn (comments, connections)
**Wednesday:** LinkedIn post — "what I'm building" or design partner update
**Thursday:** Write newsletter or blog post
**Friday:** LinkedIn post — contrarian take or industry observation

**Monthly:** 1 blog post published, 1 newsletter sent, reach out to 2 potential referral partners

## What NOT to Spend Money On (Yet)

- **Paid ads** (LinkedIn, Google, Meta) — not until revenue exceeds $5K/mo consistently
- **PR agencies** — build your own story first
- **Conferences** (as a sponsor or booth) — attend, don't sponsor
- **Branded swag** — no one needs another branded pen
- **Marketing automation tools** — free tiers are enough at this scale
- **Hiring a social media manager** — Ricardo's voice IS the brand right now

## Brand Voice in Marketing

Reference `brand/guidelines.md` for full details. Quick summary for marketing content:

- **Confident, not arrogant.** Show results, don't brag.
- **Technical but accessible.** Use plain language. If you must use jargon, explain it.
- **Practical over theoretical.** Every piece of content should leave the reader with something they can do today.
- **Personality-forward.** This is Ricardo's firm. His voice, his perspective, his experience. Don't sound like a corporation.

## Brand Positioning

### Brand Essence
Sprintt exists at the intersection of strategic thinking and rapid execution. The name communicates speed without shortcuts. The double-t is intentional — ownable, memorable, and purpose-built.

### Brand Promise
"We turn ambiguity into action and strategy into shipped results."

### Brand Pillars
- **Speed with Substance** — Move fast, but move right. Velocity comes from clarity of thinking, not cutting corners.
- **Strategy + Execution** — We don't just advise — we build. Product strategy meets operational excellence.
- **AI-Native Mindset** — Every recommendation is grounded in what AI makes possible, not what was possible five years ago.
- **Enterprise-Ready, Founder-Driven** — Big-company rigor with startup energy. Credible to executives, relatable to builders.

### Target Audience
- VP/Director-level product leaders at mid-market and enterprise companies
- Founders scaling from 0-to-1 to 1-to-N who need product operations expertise
- Companies adopting AI who need a strategic partner, not just another vendor

## Typography

### Font Stack
| Role | Font | Usage |
|------|------|-------|
| Logo | Avenir Black | Logo wordmark only — never for body or UI |
| Primary | Inter | Headlines, navigation, body, UI — everything except the logo |
| Code/Data | JetBrains Mono | Metrics, data displays, code snippets |

### Type Scale
| Element | Font | Size | Usage |
|---------|------|------|-------|
| H1 | Inter Bold | 36–40px | Page titles, hero headlines |
| H2 | Inter Semibold | 28–32px | Section headers |
| H3 | Inter Semibold | 20–24px | Subsection headers |
| Body | Inter Regular | 16–18px | Paragraphs, descriptions |
| Small | Inter Regular | 14px | Captions, metadata, fine print |
| Code | JetBrains Mono | 14–16px | Metrics, data, code blocks |

### Typography Rules
- Line height: 1.5x for body text, 1.2x for headlines
- Letter spacing: -0.02em for headlines (tighter), default for body
- Maximum line length: 65–75 characters for readability
- Never use more than 2 font weights on a single page

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Attributes
- **Confident, Not Arrogant** — State what you know clearly. No hedging with "we believe" or "we think."
- **Direct, Not Blunt** — Get to the point fast. Cut filler, avoid jargon, say what you mean. Always with warmth.
- **Technical, Not Inaccessible** — Reference frameworks and data when relevant. If an exec wouldn't get it in a boardroom, simplify.
- **Energetic, Not Manic** — Active voice, strong verbs, forward-looking language. Calm energy, not caffeine energy.

### Copy Examples
| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| We ship product strategy in weeks, not quarters. | We leverage our synergistic capabilities to optimize outcomes. |
| Your AI roadmap is unclear. Let's fix that. | We believe we might be able to potentially help with your AI journey. |
| Built 3 AI products from zero to launch. | Experienced professional with extensive background in cutting-edge technologies. |
| Here's what the data says. | In our humble opinion, we think perhaps the data might suggest... |

## Visual Style

### Design Principles
- **Whitespace is a Feature** — Generous margins, clean layouts, no clutter. Whitespace signals confidence.
- **Motion Implies Progress** — Subtle animations (fade-ins, slide-ups). Framer Motion, 0.6–0.8s durations, ease-out. Never bounce. Never overshoot.
- **Data as Design** — Large, bold, colored numbers with supporting context. Use JetBrains Mono and accent colors for metrics.
- **Consistent Radius** — 8px border radius on all UI elements (cards, buttons, inputs).

### Website Specifics
- Background: White (`#FFFFFF`) with Light Gray (`#F1F5F9`) alternating sections
- Navigation: Midnight text, Electric Blue on hover/active
- Hero section: Can use Midnight background with white text for impact
- CTAs (buttons): Electric Blue background, white text, 8px radius, bold weight
- Secondary buttons: White background, Midnight border, Midnight text
- Links: Electric Blue, no underline, underline on hover
- Card hover: Subtle shadow + slight Y-axis lift (2px)

### Icons
- Use Lucide icons (lucide.dev) throughout
- Stroke weight: 1.5–2px
- Color: inherit from text color (usually Midnight or Slate)
- Never use filled icons or emoji as UI elements

### Photography & Imagery
- Candid, natural shots over staged corporate photography
- Dark, moody tones preferred
- No cheesy handshake photos or stock meeting rooms
- Abstract tech imagery if subtle — data streams, clean interfaces, grids
- AI-generated imagery: editorial/abstract only, never photorealistic people
