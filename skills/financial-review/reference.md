# Sprintt — Business Plan

## Executive Summary

Sprintt is an AI consulting firm operated by Ricardo Ramirez under Ramirez Digital Ventures LLC, based in Freeport, Florida. The firm helps businesses close the gap between AI strategy and execution — embedding with teams to ship AI products, build workflows, and deliver measurable results.

Ricardo's competitive advantage is rare: he spans the entire software development lifecycle, has built 3 AI products from zero to launch, led product organizations from seed through Series C, and brings hands-on fluency with the full GenAI tooling stack — model-agnostic across OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and open-source. He's not just an advisor — he builds.

The delivery model is lean by design: Ricardo leads every engagement. For product development work, he contracts one SDLC engineer to cover implementation. This gives clients a complete development capability at a fraction of agency cost, while keeping Ricardo as the single accountable point of contact.

The firm is bootstrapped with minimal overhead (~$522/year in tool costs) and is targeting $50,000–$80,000 in revenue for 2026, scaling to six figures in 2027 as the client base and referral network grow.

---

## Market Opportunity

The global AI consulting market is projected to grow significantly through 2030. Key drivers:

- Businesses feel urgency to adopt AI but lack internal expertise
- 85% of enterprise AI initiatives never reach production — execution is the problem, not strategy
- Off-the-shelf AI tools require customization to deliver real value
- AI moves fast — companies need guides who also build, not just advise
- Most SMBs can't afford McKinsey; most enterprise teams can't wait for a slow agency

**Sprintt's sweet spot:** The gap between "we have an AI strategy" and "AI is working in our product."

---

## Business Model

### Delivery Model

Ricardo Ramirez leads every engagement as the primary strategist and delivery owner. For engagements requiring software development:

- Ricardo contracts **one SDLC engineer** to handle implementation
- Ricardo provides project scaffolding, agent context, and tooling setup so the contractor can move fast within a well-structured system
- Client-facing leadership, strategy, and QA remain Ricardo's responsibility at all times
- This model keeps overhead low, quality high, and margins healthy

### Revenue Streams

1. **Project-based consulting** (primary) — AI assessments, strategy workshops, AI product development
2. **Monthly retainers** (secondary, growing) — Ongoing advisory, Fractional CPO / AI Advisor engagements
3. **Training & workshops** (supplementary) — Team enablement, prompt engineering, agent tooling

### Unit Economics

- Standard hourly rate: $225/hr
- Design partner rate: $125–$150/hr
- Average project value (target): $8,000–$20,000
- Average retainer: $2,750–$5,000/mo
- Contractor cost (when applicable): ~$75–$100/hr (passed through in project pricing)
- Gross margin: ~80–88% on Ricardo-only engagements; ~65–75% on projects with SDLC contractor
- Cost of delivery: Ricardo's time + contractor hours + variable API/tool costs
