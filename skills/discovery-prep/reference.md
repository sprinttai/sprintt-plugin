## operations/discovery-call.md

# Discovery Call Framework

**Format:** 30 minutes, video preferred
**Goal:** Determine fit, understand the problem, and identify a clear next step — not sell

---

## Before the Call (5 min prep)

- Review their website and LinkedIn profile
- Note company size, industry, and what they're publicly working on
- Review which ICP they most resemble (`services/ideal-client.md`)
- Have `services/offerings.md` open in case they ask specific service questions
- Note any mutual connections or context from how they found Sprintt

---

## Call Structure

### 1. Open (2 min)

Brief intro, set expectations for the call:

> "Thanks for making time. I want to make sure this call is useful for you regardless of what we decide at the end. My goal is to understand what you're working on, share how I typically help, and figure out if there's a real fit. Sound good?"

### 2. Their World (10 min)

Let them talk. Listen for the real problem, not just the stated one.

**Core questions:**

- *"Tell me about what's going on — what made you reach out now?"*
- *"What does your team look like today? How many people are involved in [the area you want to improve]?"*
- *"What have you already tried? What worked, what didn't?"*
- *"If nothing changes in the next 6 months, what does that cost you — in time, money, or competitive ground?"*

**Listen for:**
- Specificity (good sign) vs. vagueness (may need more discovery before scoping)
- Internal vs. external blockers (people, process, or technology?)
- Timeline urgency — is this driven by a deadline or just ongoing pain?
- Budget signals — do they have a number in mind or are they fishing?

### 3. Assess Fit (5 min)

**Check the ICP criteria mentally:**
- Company size and stage?
- Decision-maker on the call?
- Clear problem with measurable impact?
- Likely budget range?

**If fit looks strong**, move toward scoping.
**If fit is unclear**, ask directly:

> *"Have you worked with outside consultants before? What did that look like budget-wise?"*
> *"Is there a budget range you're working within?"*

**Not every call will convert — and that's fine.** If they're not a fit, say so clearly and helpfully:

> *"Honestly, I don't think I'm the right fit for this one — [reason]. But I'd recommend [alternative or resource]. Happy to make an intro if that's helpful."*

### 4. Position Sprintt (5 min)

Keep this brief — anchor to what you heard, don't pitch everything.

> *"Based on what you've described, this sounds like it could be a [service type] engagement. Here's how I typically approach this..."*

Cover:
- What the engagement would look like at a high level
- What the client would have at the end
- How Ricardo personally leads every engagement
- Rough investment range (if they've signaled budget readiness)

Don't oversell. The proposal is where detail lives.

### 5. Next Steps (5 min)

Always end with a clear, specific next step — not "I'll be in touch."

**If strong fit:**
> *"I'd like to put together a proposal based on what we've discussed. I'll have it to you within 3 business days. Any additional context I should have before I start?"*

**If needs more discovery:**
> *"I want to make sure I scope this correctly. Can we schedule a 30-minute working session with [person with more context / the technical lead / your team]?"*

**If they need to think it over:**
> *"Totally makes sense. I'll send you a brief recap of what we discussed with some initial thinking. If it resonates, we can go from there."*

---

## After the Call (within 24 hours)

Send a follow-up email with:

1. **Brief summary** of what they shared (shows you listened)
2. **What you heard as the core problem** (confirms alignment)
3. **Proposed next step** (proposal, follow-up call, or intro)
4. **One clear CTA** — don't ask them to do multiple things

**Template:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Great talking today. Here's a quick recap of what I took away:
>
> **The situation:** [1–2 sentences on their problem]
>
> **What I'd propose:** [1 sentence on service type / engagement scope]
>
> **Next step:** [I'll have a proposal to you by X date / Let's schedule a follow-up with Y / etc.]
>
> Let me know if I missed anything. Otherwise I'll be in touch by [date].
>
> Ricardo

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

- No clear decision-maker on the call
- Budget is "we'll figure it out" — no range at all
- Problem is poorly defined after multiple attempts to clarify
- They want a proposal to "shop around" with no intention to decide
- Aggressive timeline with no flexibility
- Prior consultant relationship that ended badly with blame placed entirely on the consultant

These don't always mean no — but they warrant caution before investing time in a proposal.

---

## Pricing Guidance on Calls

Don't quote specific prices on the discovery call unless pressed. Anchor to ranges instead:

> *"For something like this, I typically work in the $X–$X range, depending on scope. I'll have more specifics in the proposal."*

If they push for a number before you have enough context to scope it:

> *"I don't want to give you a number that I'll have to walk back once I understand the details better. Let me put together a proper proposal — you'll have it in [X] days."*

## services/ideal-client.md

# Ideal Client Profiles

## ICP 1: The Curious SMB Owner

**Company:** 10-100 employees, $1M-$20M revenue
**Industry:** Professional services, real estate, healthcare, e-commerce, local services
**Decision maker:** Founder/CEO or COO

**Their situation:**
- Hearing about AI everywhere, feeling pressure to adopt
- Team is using ChatGPT casually but nothing systematic
- Wasting time on repetitive tasks that feel automatable
- Don't have technical staff to evaluate or implement AI

**What they need from Sprintt:**
- AI Readiness Assessment to cut through the noise
- 2-3 quick wins that show immediate ROI
- Someone who speaks business, not just tech

**How to find them:**
- Local business networks, chambers of commerce
- LinkedIn content about practical AI use cases
- Referrals from other service providers (accountants, lawyers, marketing agencies)

**Budget range:** $2,500-$10,000 per engagement

---

## ICP 2: The Growth-Stage Startup

**Company:** 5-50 employees, seed to Series B, $500K-$10M revenue
**Industry:** SaaS, fintech, healthtech, marketplace
**Decision maker:** CTO, VP Engineering, or technical co-founder

**Their situation:**
- Building a product and want to add AI capabilities
- Engineers are smart but not specialized in AI/ML
- Need to ship AI features fast to stay competitive
- Exploring AI agents, RAG systems, or custom workflows

**What they need from Sprintt:**
- Proof of concept to validate before building in-house
- Custom AI agent development
- System integration with their existing stack

**How to find them:**
- Startup communities (YC forums, Indie Hackers, local incubators)
- Tech meetups and AI events
- Twitter/X and LinkedIn tech communities
- Startup-focused Slack groups

**Budget range:** $5,000-$30,000 per engagement

---

**Company:** 100-500 employees, $20M-$200M revenue
**Industry:** Manufacturing, logistics, financial services, healthcare
**Decision maker:** VP Operations, Chief Digital Officer, or CIO

**Their situation:**
- Have budget for transformation but burned by past tech projects

**What they need from Sprintt:**

**How to find them:**

- Industry conferences and events

**Budget range:** $10,000-$50,000+ per engagement

**Decision maker:** CEO/CTO (often the same person), or technical co-founder

**Their situation:**
- Strong on engineering, weak on product strategy and prioritization
- Building features without a clear roadmap or framework for deciding what ships next
- Need senior product leadership but can't justify a full-time CPO hire yet
- May be adding AI to an existing product and need architecture + strategy, not just code

**What they need from Sprintt:**
- Fractional CPO engagement (Partner or Builder retainer)

**How to find them:**
- YC alumni networks, accelerators, and angel-backed startup communities
- LinkedIn content on AI product strategy and build-vs-buy decisions
- Referrals from investors and technical advisors in Ricardo's network

**Budget range:** $2,750–$5,000/mo (retainer); may also start with a strategy workshop

## Anti-Patterns (Clients to Avoid)

- **"Just make it cheaper" client:** Only interested in undercutting their current costs. Will nickel-and-dime every invoice.
- **"I need it yesterday" without clear scope:** Urgency without willingness to define requirements. Scope creep guaranteed.
- **"AI will fix everything" believer:** Unrealistic expectations about what AI can do. Setup for disappointment.
- **Clients who won't give access/data:** Need AI solutions but won't share the data or systems needed to build them.
- **Budget under $2,500:** Below minimum viable engagement for quality work at standard rates. Design Partner program exists for clients with real intent but tighter budgets — don't discount ad hoc, redirect.
